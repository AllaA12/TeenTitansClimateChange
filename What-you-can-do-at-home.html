What you can do at home ?



Reduce consumption and emissions at home

With small gestures, you can save energy and money, while also helping to protect the environment by reducing emissions.

Homes generate about a quarter of the direct CO2 emissions currently generated in the EU. And did you know that three quarters of the energy used by EU housing is used for heating and cooling?



Heating and cooling

 
• Do not overheat water. Set the boiler to a water heating temperature of up to 60 ° C to ensure efficient and hygienic operation.
• Program your thermostat intelligently. Did you know that your home will not heat up faster if you set the thermostat to a higher temperature than you want? The set temperature only affects the maximum temperature in the house.
• On hot days, pull the curtains and blinds to stop the sun's rays.
• Cool down with a fan. Fans consume much less than air conditioners.
insulation.
• Replace single windows with double glazed windows. Double-glazed windows lose 50-70% less heat.
• Can't mount double glazed windows? Use thick drapes or removable insulating panels to stop cold outside air.
• Do you feel current in the house? It means you lose heat. You can save energy by sealing window frames and covering under-door spaces. Even adding a lid over the keyhole (on old handles) or over the mailbox slot can help!
• Insulate hot water tanks, central heating pipes and wall cavities. If you cannot isolate the bare walls inside, install a panel that reflects the heat between the radiator and the wall.



In the kitchen

 
• Buy less power consuming equipment. In the EU, many everyday products such as refrigerators, washing machines and ovens carry the EU energy label, which helps you choose a model that consumes less. Energy efficiency is evaluated by classes, from A +++ (most efficient) to G (least efficient). For example, a Class A +++ dishwasher can only consume half the energy a Class D machine consumes.
• Check that the oven door is closed tightly. If you need to buy a new oven, choose one from class A. Throughout the life of the product, the difference in consumption between a class A and a class D oven can save up to 200 euros.
• If you have a dishwasher, do not wash the dishes by hand. Modern dishwashers consume less water and energy - and most have an "Eco" mode. However, the advantage is only felt if you fill the washing machine!
• Do not boil more water than you need. If the water has been boiling for a few minutes, you do not need to boil it again.
Wash smart
• Fill the washing machine. Don't start it for just a few clothes. Expect enough laundry to be used to its full potential, without exceeding the allowed weight.
• Choose the lowest temperature suitable for the material. Nowadays, detergents are so effective that they can clean most clothes at low temperatures.
• Do not use the pre-wash function if the laundry is not very dirty.
• Avoid using the laundry dryer as much as possible. A drying cycle can use twice as much energy as a washing cycle.


Save current
 
• Replace incandescent or halogen bulbs with less energy consuming bulbs. An LED bulb can last about 20 years, which allows you to save over 100 euros.
• Unplug your device when not in use. Smart devices connected to the internet (smart TVs, game consoles, printers) produced before 2016 can consume up to 80 watts in standby mode. Connect them to an extension cord with multiple switches and sockets to easily turn off the power.
• Ask your energy provider if you can install a smart meter. Smart gas and current metering systems can help you consume 3% less energy.
• Choose green electricity. Most energy providers offer tariffs for "green" electricity to support the development of renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar. If your home has its own roof, consider installing solar panels. This way you can generate some of the electricity you consume



Reduce consumption and emissions at the service


Wherever you work, a few simple gestures allow you to reduce your energy and resource consumption and save less.
The EU Environmental Management and Audit System ("EMAS") can help businesses and organizations in Europe and around the world to evaluate and improve their environmental performance.


• Use your own mug instead of plastic or cardboard disposable glasses. If you drink two coffees a day, that means about 500 single-use glasses a year.
• Think carefully before printing a document. Think about whether it is absolutely necessary to print a document or email, and encourage others to do the same. If really necessary, print on both sides.
• Turn off your computer and printers at night and at the weekend. Check to see if you can set your computer to economical mode.
• Buy bulk goods. Come with the suggestion that individual packets of coffee, sugar, milk and tea be replaced by large boxes, which can be used by everyone. In this way you save money and reduce the amount of waste produced.
• Try to travel less in the interest of service - organize video conferences or other remote meetings.
• Ask your hierarchical supervisor if an energy audit has been performed in the building in which you work. In buildings with lighting triggered by motion sensors, electricity bills are much lower.


Reuse and recycle!

Waste not only pollutes the air, groundwater and soil. They release CO2 and methane gas into the atmosphere, which contributes to climate change.
The amount of waste generated in the EU is increasing, currently reaching around 3 billion tonnes per year. At EU level, only 40% of household waste is currently reused or recycled.


Reuse
 
• Reuse the paper. Use the back of a used paper rather than a clean sheet of paper for a draft or shopping list.
• Donate functional old electrical equipment - for example, to schools or community centers that can reuse them.
• Purchase rechargeable batteries, which can be reused for several recharging cycles. Don't forget to recycle your spent batteries!
• Exchange, sell or donate clothing, furniture and household items that you no longer need. Instead of buying new products, you could borrow, rent or buy second-hand products.

Recycle
 
• Recycling reduces energy consumption and the volume of emissions. By recycling an aluminum can you can save 90% of the energy needed to produce a new can. If, instead of throwing away 1 kg of paper, recycle it, avoid releasing about 1 kg of CO2 (and related methane gas) into the atmosphere.
• Find out what types of waste can be recycled in your area. Do not dispose of glass, paper, cardboard, plastic and can with household waste. Recycling facilities can now handle more and more types of plastic - check locally.
• Do not dispose of electronic devices. Electronic products are made from valuable materials and resources that can be recycled. Metals, plastics, glass, all this requires a lot of energy for extraction and processing. Take them to a local collection point or to the retailer from which you bought them.
• Recycle the battery from the car when you need a new one. Old tires can also be recycled.
• Compost! Organic waste - from fruit and vegetables, eggshells or coffee grounds - is much more useful when composted than when it gets to the trash. In a box or pile of compost, exposed to the air, the waste decomposes and becomes a natural fertilizer. In a landfill where there is no air, the waste ferments and emits methane, gas with a strong greenhouse effect.

Reduce travel consumption and emissions

Walking is beneficial for you and the environment. However, we have to use the means of transport - to get to work, to visit our relatives or friends, to travel. This is why it is important to make the right choices so that we generate as little CO2 as possible.
The transport sector generates almost a quarter of all EU greenhouse gas emissions. Did you know that over half of the CO2 emissions directly generated by European citizens, at home or outside the home, are generated by their own transport (commuting to work by car, traveling by plane to the holiday destination, etc.)?

Choose wisely

 
• Leave your car at home. For every liter of fuel burned in the engine of the car, more than 2 kg of CO2 is released. This means that on average, using a fuel tank, you release over 100 kg of CO2 into the atmosphere!
• Alternatives: bicycle, public transport and co-travel - car-pooling or car-sharing in cars. Of course, walking is the most environmentally friendly mode of transport!
• Take the train. A person traveling alone by car generates 3 times more CO2 emissions per km than if they were traveling by train.
• Find alternatives when traveling by plane. Air transport is the transport sector with the highest growth rate of CO2 emissions. For relatively short distances (up to several hundred kilometers), you prefer the train journey.
If you still need to take the car ...
 
• The way you drive has a major impact on fuel consumption and the amount of CO2 emitted. It is better to drive at a relatively constant speed, than to accelerate strongly and to brake suddenly on the same course. Keep the recommended pressure on the wheels - deflated tires increase fuel consumption.
• Remove the trunk support bars fixed to the roof of the car, if not in use. They increase fuel consumption and CO2 emissions by up to 10%, because they reduce the aerodynamics of the car.
• Want to buy a new car? Choose a low-power one. All car manufacturers and dealers must display CO2 emissions and fuel consumption of vehicles in car shows and advertising materials in the EU.
Responsible travelers
 
• Think about "offsetting" the CO2 emissions of your trip. Many transport companies and organizations calculate the footprint of the emissions you generate by traveling and invest an appropriate amount in environmental or energy production projects from renewable sources, for example.
• Do you like the idea of ​​ecotourism? See the online Catalog of tourist establishments with the EU eco-label

Reduce the impact on the environment FOR SHOPS AND AT THE TABLE

Many Europeans now have access to a wide variety of foods and products. But everything we buy, from a liter of milk to a pair of new shoes, needs to be produced - and this generates CO2 emissions. In addition, many production processes are environmentally harmful and accelerate climate change.
The transport of food products by plane from one corner of the world to another generates 1,700 times more CO2 emissions than their transport by truck over a distance of 50 km. The unreasonable exploitation of forests contributes to deforestation, generating about 20% of global CO2 emissions.

Make smart shopping choices!


 
• Attention to packaging: Plastic packaging creates large amounts of waste and is often difficult to recycle. Try to avoid packaged goods and look for reserves for products such as shampoo.
• When you go shopping, take your reusable bags. Buy toilet paper and kitchen towels from recycled paper.
• Think twice before changing your phone or tablet. Waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE) is the fastest growing waste category in the EU.
• Want to buy garden furniture or other wood products? Look for FSC or PEFC labels, which guarantee that the wood has been used in a sustainable way.

Eat smart!
 
• Do not waste food. Just buy as much as you need and keep the scraps for later.
• Avoid non-seasonal produce - growing plants in a different environment than their natural environment or in a season other than their natural growing season requires huge consumption of water, energy and other resources.
• Wash fruits and vegetables in a bowl rather than under a stream of water. Water the plants with the remaining water.
• Replace some of the red meat you eat with poultry, fish or legumes. Switching from beef to poultry can reduce your carbon footprint of the meal served by up to 75%.


Ecological label


• Ecological label of products identified by the EU and services with low impact on the environment. Search for this logo for the packaging of bulbs, detergents and computers and for the label for both the care you buy. Even when booking a hotel room, it is checked whether the hotel is the EU's first eco-friendly label. Currently, there are approximately 70,000 products and services bearing the EU eco-label!
