Climate changes


The causes
of climate change 


The influence humans have on the Planet's climate and temperature change has
increased,burning fossil fuels,by cutting down tropical forests and raising animals.

These activities generate huge quantities of greenhouse gases, which are added to
those already naturally present in the atmosphere,
thus contributing to the greenhouse effect and to global warming.


 

Greenhouse gases

Some gases from the Earth's atmosphere behave like the walls of a greenhouse - they capture and retain the heat of the sun,so that it is no longer released back into space.

Many of them were naturally in the atmosphere, but human activity has increased the concentration of several, especially:

-Carbon dioxide (CO2)
 -Methane
 -Oxide

-Of fluorinated gases

CO2 is the gas that is most often generated by human activities through its greenhouse effect, being responsible for 63% of the global warming caused by man.
Other greenhouse gases are emitted into the atmosphere in smaller quantities, but they capture and retain heat more efficiently than CO2,
and in some cases are thousands of times stronger.
Methane contributes 19% to global warming caused by man, and nitrogen protoxide by 6%.

Causes of increased emissions :

-Combustion of coal, oil and gas generates carbon dioxide and nitrous oxide.
-Deforestation
Trees contribute to regulating climatic conditions by absorbing CO2 from the atmosphere. Therefore, when cut, this beneficial effect is lost and the carbon dioxide stored by the trees is released back into the atmosphere, accentuating the greenhouse effect.
-Intensive breeding of animals
Cattle and sheep produce large amounts of methane during digestion.
- Fertilizers containing nitrogen generate nitrous oxide emissions.
-Fluorinated gases have a very strong heating effect, up to 23 000 times higher than CO2.Fortunately, they are released in smaller quantities, and EU law provides for a gradual reduction of their use, until completely
eliminated.

Global warming

  


At present, the global average temperature is 0.85C higher than at the end of the 19th century. Each of the last three decades has been warmer than any previous decade for which there are records ( from 1850 to present).

Climate experts from around the world believe that human activities are almost certainly the main cause of global warming observed since the mid-20th century.

A 2 C rise in temperature over the pre-industrial period is considered by scientists as the threshold beyond which there is a much higher risk of witnessing dangerous and potentially catastrophic climate change globally. For this reason, the international community has announced that it is necessary to maintain global warming below the 2 C threshold.


The consequences of climate change


Climate change affects all regions of the world.
The icebergs are melting, and the level of the seas and oceans is rising. In
some regions, extreme weather and precipitation are becoming more frequent,
while others are experiencing extreme heat and drought.



 

Melting ice and raising sea levels

When the water heats up, it increases its volume. Global warming is also at the origin of melting the ice cap.

Taken together, these changes lead to increased levels of seas and oceans and, thus, flooding and erosion of coastal and lowland areas


        

       

                       


Extreme weather  events,  rainfall regime change.


The heavy rain and other extreme weather events are becoming more and more common. As a result of this situation, the are more floods and the quality of the water is dropping, and water ressources are harder to obtain in some regions.


                

                                                   

The consequences for Europe.


 - Central and South Europe is dealing with more frequent heat waves, forest fires and drought.

- The Mediterranean zone is becoming more and more dry, which makes it more vulnerable to drought and forest fires.

- The northern part of Europe is becoming considerably wetter and it is possible that flooding during winter may become a common event

- The urban zone, where 4 out of 5 europeans live right now, are affected by heat waves, floods or increased sea level rise, and often dont have the requirements to adapt to such climate changes.

The consequences for countries that are developing.

The population of many countries that are developing poorly are the most affected. besides the fact that their lives depend often in a very large measure of the natural environment, they have the least resources that could allow them to face the climate changes.



 

                                  The dangers for human health.

The climate change has already impacted the human health.

-More people have died because of heat in some regions, and the ones number of those that died of cold has decreased.

-We are already witnessing the change of distribution of some diseases that are transmitted using water or vectors.


   






Costs for society and economy.


The damage caused to immovable property and infrastructure, but also to human health entails high costs for the society and economy.

Between 1980-2011, over 5.5 million people were affected by floods, and the direct economical losses resulted were over 90 billion euros

Areas that highly depend on the temperature and weather, like agriculture, forestry, energy and tourism, are particularly affected.


 







Dangers for the wild flora and fauna


The climate changes are happening so fast that the survival of some species of plants and animals is in danger. Many terrestrial, freshwater and marine species have already migrated. Some species of plants and animals are also in danger to become extinct if the temperature of the global environments keep rising unchecked.
